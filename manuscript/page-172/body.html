Just before eight he approached the deserted earth-work and entered by the south path which descended over the debris of the former dens. In a few moments he could discern a female figure creeping in by the great north gap, or public gateway. They met in the middle of the arena. Neither spoke just at first—there was no necessity for speech—and the poor woman leant against Henchard, who supported her in his arms.

“I don't drink,” he said in a low, halting, apologetic voice. “You hear, Susan?—I don't drink now—I haven't since that night.” Those were his first words.

He felt her bow her head in acknowledgment that she understood. After a minute or two he again began:
