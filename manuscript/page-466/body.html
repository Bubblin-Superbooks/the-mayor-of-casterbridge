“Why did you come here to find me, then?”

“I thought I ought to marry you for conscience' sake, since you were free, even though I—did not like you so well.”

“And why then don't you think so now?”

She was silent. It was only too obvious that conscience had ruled well enough till new love had intervened and usurped that rule. In feeling this she herself forgot for the moment her partially justifying argument—that having discovered Henchard's infirmities of temper, she had some excuse for not risking her happiness in his hands after once escaping them.
